Types of Man's Best Friend

There are many types of Man's best friend and they each have their own types of personalities. Depending on what you are looking for in a best friend you can find a dog that matches your needs. Some dogs are independent and aloof, some dogs are protective and loyal, some dogs are affectionate. There are dogs who are very gentle with children. Some dogs are easy to groom, some dogs are easy to train. There are dogs with a high energy level. There are dogs who are friendly towards strangers. There are dogs who are especially intelligent. Depending on what you are looking for in your best friend you can a perfect match.

  1. Labrador Retriever
    There are two types of Labradors - the English Labrador and the American Labrador. English labs are heavier and thicker than American labs. Americans labs are tall and lanky with a smooth coat. Their coat color comes in black, yellow or chocolate. The labrador is one the most popular breeds in America. The Labrador Retriever dog is loyal, loving and affectionate. They are very inteligent and want to please. The typical male Labrador Retriever is 22-24 inches and 60-70 pounds. Labrador Retrievers love to play in the water - an excellent way to make sure they excercise. The life expectancy of a Labrador is ten to twelve years.
  2. German Shepard
    A German Shepherd dog is a strong, muscular dog. They are courageous, alert and fearless. German Shepherds are obedient and willing to learn. A German Shepard is one of the smartest, most trainable breeds. German Shepards love their family and have a strong protective instinct. German Shepards average 24-26 inches and weigh 77-85 pounds. Their life expectancy is 13 years. The most common color is black with tan.
  3. Golden Retriever
    The Golden Retriever is a medium sized dog. The coat comes in cream to a rich golden color. They are loveable, well mannered, intelligent dogs. Golden Retrievers are friendly and make excellent family dogs. They want to please their master and do well with obedience training. The typical male grows to 22-24 inches and will weigh 60 to 80 pounds. A Golden Retriever will live 10-12 years.
  4. Bulldog
    Their are five varities of Bulldogs - English, Australian, French, Victorian and American. The English Bulldog is a wide, medium size dog with short legs. They come in many colors - both solid or a combination of two colors. Bulldogs are determined, persistant dogs. They are known for their guarding abilities. They are good family pets. Bulldogs snore loudly, they drool, slobber and are messy eaters. The average male Bulldog will grow to 12-16 inches and will weigh 53-55 pounds. Unless the Bulldog gets daily excercise they may develop behavior issues. Average life for a Bulldog is 8 years.
  5. Beagle
    A Beagle is a strong, squarely built, medium sized dog. A Beagle is a type of hound dog and comes in a variety of colors. They can be black and tan, red and white, orange and white, lemon and white. Beagles have a distinctive howl when they are hunting. A Beagle is a loving, happy dog. They are very social and excellent with children. They have hunting instincts and are curious dogs. Beagles only grow to 14 to 16 inches and will weigh 22-25 pounds. They will live 12-15 years. Beagles are energetic and have great stamina.
  6. Yorkshire Terrier
    A Yorkshire Terrier is a small, toy-sized dog. The coat on a Yorkshire Terrier is fine and silky. The hair on top of a Yorkie is so long it has to be tied up so the dog can see. Yorkshire Terriers act bigger than their size. They are energetic, brave and loyal. Yorkies are easy to train and are affectionate. Yorkies have to be trained properly or they become demanding and dependent or yappy. Yorkshire Terriers are only 6 - 7 inches typically and weigh 7 pounds. They will live 12-15 years. Because Yorkshire Terriers are so active it is important they get a daily walk.
  7. Rottweiler
    Rottweilers are muscular, powerful dogs. Most Rottweiler's eyes are dark but some Rottweiler's have blue eyes or one blue and one brown. The Rottweilers coat is short and thick. Most Rottweilers are black with rust markings. The Rottweiler is a devoted dog. They love their family and are very loyal and protective. The Rottweiler is very intelligent. The typical male is 24-27 inches and will weigh 95-130 pounds. The Rottweiler will live 10-12 years. The more excercise you give a Rottweiler the happier he will be.
  8. Siberian Husky
    A Siberian Husky is a strong, stocky, working sled dog. Their coat is thick and can withstand temperatures as low as minus 58 degrees. They have hair between their toes to help them keep warm. Siberian Huskies are gentle, playful and loving dogs. They are very social and love thier families. Siberian Huskies are good with children. The typical male husky will be 21-23 inches and weigh 45-60 pounds. The Siberian Husky will live about 12-16 years.
  9. Great Dane
    A Great Dane is a giant, powerful dog. The Great Dane is often called a "gentle giant." They are playful, patient and affectionate. A Great Dane loves to be around people. The Great Dane does not bark much. The Great Dane is a very dependable dog. The typical male Great Dane grows 30-34 inches and weighs between 120-200 pounds. Because of it's size the Great Dane has to be taught not to jump on people. Most Great Danes only live about 10 years. They need plenty of excercise and should be taken on daily walks.
  10. Cocker Spaniel
    The Cocker Spaniel is a medium sized dog. The Cocker Spaniel's hair is medium length on the body but short and fine on the head. Cocker Spaniels love to work. They also make good pets. A Cocker Spaniel has average intelligence and is playful, gentle and sweet. Cocker Spaniels are good with children. The typical male grows 15 inches and weighs 15-30 pounds. A Cocker Spaniel lives between 12-15 years. Cocker Spaniels are strong and need regular excercise.
